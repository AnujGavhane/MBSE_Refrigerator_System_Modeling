# Refrigerator Circuit Components

## Thermostat

The thermostat senses the refrigerator's internal temperature and automatically switches the compressor ON or OFF to maintain the user-selected cooling level.

**MBSE Function:** Temperature Control

---

## Compressor

The compressor is the primary cooling component. It compresses the R134a refrigerant and circulates it through the refrigeration cycle to remove heat from the refrigerator.

**MBSE Function:** Cooling System

---

## Overload Protector (OLP)

The OLP protects the compressor from excessive current and overheating by disconnecting power during abnormal operating conditions.

**MBSE Function:** Safety & Protection

---

## PTC Starter

The Positive Temperature Coefficient (PTC) starter provides the high starting current required by the compressor motor and automatically disconnects the start winding after startup.

**MBSE Function:** Motor Starting

---

## Run Capacitor

The run capacitor improves motor performance, starting torque, and operational efficiency during compressor operation.

**MBSE Function:** Electrical Support

---

## Door Switch

The door switch detects whether the refrigerator door is open or closed and controls the interior lamp accordingly.

**MBSE Function:** User Interaction

---

## Interior Lamp

Provides illumination inside the refrigerator whenever the door is opened.

**MBSE Function:** User Interface

---

## AC Power Supply

The refrigerator operates from a **220–240 V AC, 50 Hz** supply, providing electrical energy to all subsystems.

**MBSE Function:** Power Source

---

## Earth Connection

The protective earth connection safeguards users from electrical shock by providing a safe path for fault currents.

**MBSE Function:** Electrical Safety

---

# Relation to MBSE

The electrical circuit represents the implementation layer of the refrigerator system. While SysML models define the system's requirements, behavior, structure, and interactions, the circuit diagram illustrates how these functions are physically realized using electrical components. Studying both perspectives establishes traceability between the logical system model and the actual hardware implementation, which is a key objective of Model-Based Systems Engineering (MBSE).
